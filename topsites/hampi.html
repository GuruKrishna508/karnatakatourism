Ruins of the Magnificent Vijayanagara Empire

Historical Heritage

Hampi, the crown jewel of Karnataka and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, stands as a testament to the grandeur of the Vijayanagara Empire. Once the second-largest medieval city in the world after Beijing, Hampi flourished as the empire's capital from 1336 to 1565 CE. The sprawling ruins spread across 4,100 hectares showcase magnificent temples, royal complexes, and ancient marketplaces carved from granite boulders. The iconic Virupaksha Temple, still active after 700 years, and the stunning Vittala Temple with its famous stone chariot represent the pinnacle of Dravidian architecture. Every stone in Hampi tells a story of artistic brilliance, religious devotion, and architectural innovation that continues to mesmerize visitors from around the globe.

Places to Explore

Virupaksha Temple

The oldest functioning temple in Hampi, dedicated to Lord Shiva, featuring a towering gopuram and intricate carvings. A living shrine that has witnessed centuries of devotion and history.

Vittala Temple Complex

Home to the famous stone chariot and musical pillars, this architectural masterpiece showcases the zenith of Vijayanagara craftsmanship with its intricate sculptures and innovative design.

Hampi Bazaar

The ancient marketplace that once buzzed with merchants from across the world, featuring a long colonnade of ruined structures that speak of Hampi's commercial glory.

Royal Enclosure

The fortified area containing the remnants of royal palaces, including the Lotus Mahal, Elephant Stables, and the Queen's Bath, showcasing royal architectural grandeur.

Matanga Hill

The highest point in Hampi offering breathtaking sunrise and sunset views over the boulder-strewn landscape and ancient ruins, perfect for photography and meditation.

Tungabhadra River

The sacred river that nurtured the Vijayanagara civilization, offering coracle rides and riverside temples, creating a serene contrast to the ancient stone structures.
