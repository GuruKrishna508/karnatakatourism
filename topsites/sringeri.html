Spiritual Heritage

Sringeri, nestled in the lush Western Ghats of Karnataka, stands as one of India's most revered spiritual and educational centers. Founded in the 8th century CE by Adi Shankaracharya, this sacred town is home to the first of the four cardinal mathas (monasteries) established by the great philosopher-saint. The Sringeri Sharada Peetham has been a beacon of Vedantic learning and spiritual wisdom for over 1,200 years, maintaining an unbroken lineage of Shankaracharyas who have guided seekers on the path of knowledge and devotion. Located on the banks of the pristine Tunga River, Sringeri derives its name from the legend of a serpent (Sringa) providing shade to a pregnant frog, symbolizing the town's ethos of compassion and protection. The magnificent Sharada Temple, dedicated to the goddess of learning, and the Vidyashankara Temple, with its unique zodiacal architecture, stand as testimonies to the town's rich cultural and architectural heritage. The peaceful ambiance, combined with the continuous chanting of Vedic hymns and the scholarly discussions that have echoed through centuries, makes Sringeri a living repository of ancient Indian wisdom and spiritual tradition.

Sacred Places

Sharada Temple

The main temple dedicated to Goddess Sharada, the deity of learning and wisdom. This ancient temple houses a magnificent gold-plated idol of the goddess and serves as the primary place of worship for devotees seeking knowledge and enlightenment. The temple's serene atmosphere and divine presence make it a must-visit for spiritual seekers.

Vidyashankara Temple

An architectural marvel built in 1338 CE, this temple uniquely combines Hoysala and Dravidian architectural styles. The temple features twelve intricately carved pillars representing the zodiac signs, and the sanctum is positioned such that the sun's rays fall on different pillars during different months, showcasing ancient astronomical knowledge.

Sringeri Sharada Peetham

The principal monastery established by Adi Shankaracharya, serving as the seat of learning and spiritual guidance for over twelve centuries. The Peetham houses ancient manuscripts, conducts Vedic studies, and maintains the sacred traditions of Advaita Vedanta philosophy under the guidance of the Shankaracharya.

Tunga River

The sacred river that flows through Sringeri, considered highly auspicious by devotees. The river's pristine waters and peaceful banks provide a perfect setting for meditation and spiritual contemplation. The evening aarti (prayer ceremony) performed on the riverbank is a deeply moving spiritual experience.

Shankaracharya Samadhi

The memorial site marking the final resting place of the successive Shankaracharyas. This sacred spot serves as a place of reverence and reflection, where devotees pay homage to the great spiritual masters who have preserved and propagated the ancient wisdom of Vedanta through the ages.

Sivaratri Car Festival

An annual celebration that transforms Sringeri into a vibrant center of devotion and cultural activity. The festival features the ceremonial chariot procession of the deities, accompanied by traditional music, dance, and spiritual discourses, drawing thousands of devotees from across the country.
