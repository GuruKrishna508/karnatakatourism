Coffee Heritage

Coorg, officially known as Kodagu, is a picturesque hill station in Karnataka famous for its sprawling coffee plantations, spice gardens, and lush green landscapes. Known as the "Scotland of India," this enchanting region is home to the warrior Kodava community and their rich cultural traditions. The rolling hills covered in coffee estates, cascading waterfalls, and misty mornings create a perfect retreat for nature lovers. Coorg's coffee heritage dates back to the 1600s, making it one of India's premier coffee-growing regions, producing some of the finest Arabica and Robusta beans.

Places to Explore

Abbey Falls

A spectacular waterfall cascading from a height of 70 feet, surrounded by lush coffee plantations and spice estates in a serene natural setting.

Raja's Seat

A beautiful garden offering panoramic views of the valley and mountains, especially stunning during sunrise and sunset with its musical fountain display.

Coffee Plantations

Experience guided tours through aromatic coffee estates, learn about coffee cultivation, and enjoy fresh brews amidst the plantation atmosphere.

Talakaveri

The sacred birthplace of River Kaveri, located on Brahmagiri hills, offering spiritual significance and breathtaking views of the Western Ghats.

Namdroling Monastery

The largest Tibetan settlement in South India, featuring magnificent golden Buddhist temples and peaceful monasteries in Bylakuppe.

Dubare Elephant Camp

An interactive experience with elephants on the banks of River Kaveri, offering elephant rides, bathing sessions, and wildlife encounters.
