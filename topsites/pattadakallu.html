Welcome to Pattadakallu

The Crown Jewel of Chalukyan Architecture

Architectural Heritage

Pattadakallu, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, stands as a testament to the architectural brilliance of the Chalukyan dynasty in Karnataka, India. Located on the banks of the Malaprabha River, this ancient temple complex represents the pinnacle of South Indian temple architecture from the 7th and 8th centuries CE. The site contains ten magnificent temples that showcase the evolution of temple architecture, blending both Dravidian and Nagara styles. The most prominent among these is the Virupaksha Temple, built in 740 CE by Queen Lokamahadevi to commemorate her husband Vikramaditya II's victory over the Pallavas. The intricate stone carvings, sculptural masterpieces, and architectural innovations at Pattadakallu influenced temple construction across South India for centuries. Each temple tells a story of devotion, artistry, and cultural synthesis, making it one of India's most important archaeological sites. The complex serves as an open-air museum of ancient Indian architecture, where visitors can witness the transition from early Chalukyan to later medieval temple styles.

Architectural Marvels

Virupaksha Temple

The grandest temple in the complex, built in 740 CE by Queen Lokamahadevi. This magnificent structure showcases exquisite Dravidian architecture with intricate carvings depicting scenes from the Ramayana and Mahabharata, making it a masterpiece of ancient Indian temple art.

Mallikarjuna Temple

A stunning example of Chalukyan architecture, this temple was also built by Queen Lokamahadevi. It features beautiful sculptures of dancing figures, mythological scenes, and intricate floral patterns that demonstrate the artistic excellence of the period.

Sangameshvara Temple

The earliest temple in the complex, begun by Vijayaditya and completed by Vikramaditya II. This temple represents the initial phase of Chalukyan architecture and provides insight into the evolution of South Indian temple design and construction techniques.

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

A unique temple that showcases the Northern (Nagara) style of architecture, demonstrating the cultural exchange and architectural experimentation that took place at Pattadakallu. It features a distinctive curvilinear tower and ornate decorative elements.

Galaganatha Temple

Another example of Northern style architecture, this temple highlights the synthesis of different architectural traditions. The temple features beautiful sculptural work and represents the cosmopolitan nature of Chalukyan artistic expression.

Archaeological Museum

Houses a collection of sculptures, inscriptions, and artifacts from the Pattadakallu complex. The museum provides detailed information about the historical significance, architectural features, and cultural importance of this UNESCO World Heritage Site.
