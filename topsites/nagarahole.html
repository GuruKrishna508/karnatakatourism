Wilderness Heritage

Nagarahole National Park, also known as Rajiv Gandhi National Park, is one of India's premier wildlife destinations located in the picturesque Kodagu and Mysuru districts of Karnataka. This magnificent wilderness sanctuary spans over 847 square kilometers and forms part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve. Established in 1988, Nagarahole is renowned for its diverse ecosystem that supports a rich variety of flora and fauna. The park is home to the majestic Bengal tiger, Asian elephants, leopards, wild dogs, and over 270 species of birds. The Kabini River meanders through the park, creating lush riverside forests and grasslands that provide perfect habitat for wildlife. Nagarahole's landscape varies from moist deciduous forests to bamboo groves and swamplands, making it a photographer's paradise and nature lover's dream. The park offers excellent safari experiences with opportunities to spot wildlife in their natural habitat, making it one of the best places in India for tiger sightings and elephant encounters.

Wildlife Attractions

Tiger Safari

Experience the thrill of spotting the magnificent Bengal tigers in their natural habitat. Nagarahole is known for its high tiger density and offers excellent opportunities for tiger sightings during morning and evening safaris through dense forests and grasslands.

Kabini River

The lifeline of Nagarahole, the Kabini River provides scenic boat safaris where you can observe elephants, deer, and various bird species along the riverbanks. The river creates a stunning backdrop for wildlife photography and peaceful nature experiences.

Elephant Encounters

Witness large herds of Asian elephants roaming freely through the park. Nagarahole is famous for its elephant population, and visitors can observe these gentle giants bathing in the river and grazing in the grasslands during safari drives.

Bird Watching Paradise

Home to over 270 species of birds including the Malabar Pied Hornbill, Crested Serpent Eagle, and various kingfishers. The diverse habitats within the park make it an excellent destination for both amateur and professional bird watchers.

Kabini Dam Backwaters

The scenic backwaters formed by the Kabini Dam offer boat safaris and provide excellent opportunities to spot wildlife coming to drink water. The calm waters reflect the surrounding forests, creating picture-perfect moments for nature enthusiasts.

Luxury Wildlife Resorts

Stay at world-class eco-friendly resorts that offer comfortable accommodation while maintaining harmony with nature. These resorts provide guided safaris, nature walks, and educational programs about wildlife conservation.
