Welcome to Bandipur

Discover India's premier wildlife sanctuary in the Western Ghats

Wildlife Heritage

Bandipur National Park, established in 1974, is one of India's most renowned tiger reserves and wildlife sanctuaries. Located in Karnataka's Chamarajanagar district, this 874 square kilometer paradise is part of the larger Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve. Home to the majestic Bengal tiger, Asian elephants, leopards, and over 200 bird species, Bandipur represents the rich biodiversity of the Western Ghats. The park's diverse ecosystems, from dry deciduous forests to grasslands, create a perfect habitat for wildlife conservation and nature enthusiasts.

Wildlife Experiences

Tiger Safari

Experience the thrill of spotting the magnificent Bengal tiger in its natural habitat during guided jungle safaris through dense forests and grasslands.

Elephant Encounters

Witness large herds of Asian elephants roaming freely across the park, especially during early morning and evening hours.

Bird Watching

Discover over 200 bird species including the Malabar Trogon, Indian Roller, and various raptors in their pristine forest environment.

Himavad Gopalaswamy Betta

Visit the highest peak in the Bandipur hills, offering panoramic views and a historic temple surrounded by lush forests.

Moyar River

Explore the scenic river that forms the northern boundary of the park, attracting diverse wildlife and offering excellent photography opportunities.

Nature Interpretation Centre

Learn about the park's ecology, wildlife conservation efforts, and the importance of preserving natural habitats for future generations.
