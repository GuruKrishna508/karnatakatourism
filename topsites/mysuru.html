The City of Palaces - Where Royal Legacy Lives On

Royal Heritage

Mysuru, formerly known as Mysore, is a magnificent city in Karnataka that served as the capital of the Kingdom of Mysore for nearly six centuries. This enchanting destination is renowned for its opulent palaces, rich cultural heritage, and royal grandeur. The crown jewel of Mysuru is the spectacular Mysore Palace, one of India's most visited monuments, which showcases Indo-Saracenic architecture with its ornate domes, arches, and elaborate decorations. The city is famous for its vibrant Dasara festival, where the palace is illuminated with thousands of lights, creating a magical spectacle. Mysuru has been a center of learning, art, and culture, producing exquisite silk sarees, sandalwood products, and incense. The city's royal legacy is evident in its well-planned streets, beautiful gardens, and architectural marvels. From the summer palace of Tipu Sultan to the sacred Chamundeshwari Temple atop Chamundi Hill, Mysuru offers a perfect blend of history, spirituality, and royal splendor that continues to captivate visitors from around the world.

Royal Destinations

Mysore Palace

The magnificent Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is a stunning example of Indo-Saracenic architecture. This opulent palace features intricate carvings, beautiful paintings, and is illuminated with thousands of lights during special occasions.

Chamundi Hill

Sacred hill topped with the ancient Chamundeshwari Temple, offering panoramic views of Mysuru city. The hill features the massive Nandi Bull statue and provides a spiritual retreat with scenic beauty.

Brindavan Gardens

Spectacular terrace gardens built below the Krishnarajasagara Dam, famous for its musical fountain, colorful illumination, and symmetrical design. A perfect evening destination for families and couples.

Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace

Also known as Daria Daulat Bagh, this historic palace showcases the grandeur of Tipu Sultan's reign. The palace features beautiful frescoes, artifacts, and provides insights into the Tiger of Mysore's life.

St. Philomena's Cathedral

One of the largest churches in India, built in neo-Gothic style with twin spires reaching 175 feet. This magnificent cathedral houses relics of Saint Philomena and stunning stained glass windows.

Devaraja Market

Vibrant traditional market famous for Mysore silk, sandalwood products, spices, and flowers. This bustling bazaar offers an authentic local shopping experience with aromatic spices and colorful displays.
