Welcome to Jog Falls

India's Second Highest Waterfall - Nature's Spectacular Display

Natural Wonder

Jog Falls, also known as Gerosoppa Falls or Joga Falls, is a magnificent waterfall located in the Western Ghats of Karnataka. Standing at a breathtaking height of 253 meters (830 feet), it ranks as India's second-highest plunge waterfall and one of the most spectacular natural wonders in the country. Created by the Sharavathi River taking a dramatic plunge over the rocky cliffs, Jog Falls is renowned for its four distinct cascades: Raja, Rani, Rover, and Rocket. During the monsoon season, the falls transform into a thunderous spectacle of nature's raw power, with millions of gallons of water cascading down the precipice, creating a mesmerizing mist that can be seen from miles away. The surrounding tropical rainforest and biodiversity make it a paradise for nature lovers and photographers.

Places to Explore

Main Waterfall Viewpoint

The primary viewing platform offering spectacular panoramic views of all four cascades. Best visited during monsoon when the falls are at their most powerful and magnificent.

Sharavathi Valley

The lush green valley surrounding the falls, perfect for nature walks and wildlife spotting. Home to diverse flora and fauna typical of the Western Ghats ecosystem.

Linganamakki Dam

The upstream dam that controls the water flow to Jog Falls. During peak monsoon, the dam releases excess water, creating the spectacular cascade that attracts thousands of visitors.

Tunga Anicut Dam

A historic dam built across the Tunga River, offering scenic views and engineering marvels. It's an important irrigation project that supports agriculture in the region.

Ikkeri

A nearby historical town featuring ancient Keladi Nayaka architecture, including temples and palaces that showcase the region's rich cultural heritage and artistic traditions.

Sakrebailu Elephant Camp

A nearby elephant training camp where visitors can observe and interact with elephants in their natural habitat, offering unique wildlife experiences and conservation insights.
