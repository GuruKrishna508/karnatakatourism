Explore the ancient rock-cut temples of Karnataka's historic capital

Heritage

Badami, once the capital of the Early Chalukyas, is known for its magnificent rock-cut cave temples carved into sandstone cliffs. Dating back to the 6th century, these temples feature intricate carvings of deities like Shiva, Vishnu, and Jain Tirthankaras. The site reflects a fusion of North and South Indian architectural styles and showcases the Chalukyan contribution to Hindu temple evolution.

Places to See

Badami Cave Temples

Four major cave temples carved into sandstone cliffs, dedicated to Hindu and Jain deities, known for their artistic and religious significance.

Agastya Lake

A serene lake that offers a picturesque view of the temples and the rugged landscape of Badami.

Bhutanatha Temple

A beautiful temple on the lake shore, offering reflections in the water and a tranquil atmosphere.

Badami Fort

Situated on a hilltop, the fort provides panoramic views of the region and insight into military architecture.

Archaeological Museum

This museum displays a collection of sculptures, inscriptions, and artifacts excavated from Badami and nearby sites.

Malegitti Shivalaya

A 7th-century Shiva temple built on a rocky outcrop, offering sweeping views of the landscape below.
