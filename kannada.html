Kannada - The Language of Karnataka

ಕನ್ನಡದ ಇತಿಹಾಸ - History of Kannada

Kannada is one of the oldest languages in India, with a literary tradition spanning over 1,500 years. It belongs to the Dravidian family of languages and is the official language of Karnataka state.

The language has evolved through several stages:

450-1200 CE
Old Kannada (Haḷegannaḍa) - The earliest form found in inscriptions
1200-1700 CE
Middle Kannada (Naḍugannaḍa) - Classical literary period
1700-Present
Modern Kannada (Hosagannaḍa) - Contemporary form

ಹಳ್ಮಿಡಿ ಶಾಸನ - Halmidi Inscription

ಕನ್ನಡದ ಪ್ರಾಚೀನ ಸಾಕ್ಷಿ

The Halmidi Inscription is one of the earliest known inscriptions in the Kannada language, dating back to approximately 450 CE. This ancient stone inscription was discovered in Halmidi village in the Hassan district of Karnataka.

This remarkable archaeological find provides crucial evidence of the antiquity of the Kannada script and language. The inscription is written in the pre-old Kannada script and contains references to land grants and administrative details of the time.

Significance of Halmidi Inscription

• Oldest known Kannada inscription
• Written in 5th century CE
• Contains 6 lines of ancient Kannada
• Mentions King Kakusthavarma
• Preserved in Government Museum, Bangalore

ಕನ್ನಡದ ವೈಭವ - Glory of Kannada

ಸಾಹಿತ್ಯ ಸಂಪದ - Literary Heritage

Kannada literature boasts a rich tradition with works like Kavirajamarga (850 CE), the earliest available work on rhetoric and poetics in Kannada. The language has produced legendary poets like Pampa, Ranna, and Ponna, known as the "Ratnatraya" (Three Gems) of Kannada literature.

ಜ್ಞಾನಪೀಠ ಪುರಸ್ಕಾರ - Jnanpith Awards

Kannada literature has been honored with 8 Jnanpith Awards, India's highest literary honor, making it one of the most celebrated regional literatures in the country.

ಭಾಷೆಯ ವಿಶಿಷ್ಟತೆ - Language Features

Kannada script, derived from the Brahmi script, has 49 characters - 13 vowels and 36 consonants. The language is known for its phonetic nature, where words are pronounced exactly as they are written.
